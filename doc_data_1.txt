## 🎨 UI/UX Design System: "Cinematic Accessibility"


### A. The "Living History" Dashboard (Home)
- **Hero Section:** A slow-motion, high-quality background video of *the user's own uploaded content* or generic "Americana/Nature" b-roll (Nat Geo style).
- **The "Big Button" Interface:**
    - **[ Tell a Story ]** (Triggers the Voice Interview Engine)
    - **[ Watch My Life ]** (Plays the generated documentary)
    - **[ Go Live ]** (Simple "On Air" button for family streaming)


### B. The "Interview" Engine (Voice-to-Video)
- **Concept:** "The AI Director." The user receives a simulated phone call.
- **Visuals:**
    - **Senior View:** Simple "In Call" screen. Large photos of the topic (e.g., if talking about 1960s, show 1960s photos).
    - **Feedback:** "I can hear you perfectly" (Green waveform).
- **Output:** The engine stitches the audio with stock footage (History Channel style) automatically.


### C. The "Generational Bridge" (Youth View)
- **Toggle:** "Family Mode" vs. "Creator Mode."
- **Creator Mode:** Allows grandchildren to take the AI-generated documentary and apply "TikTok Style" edits (fast cuts, captions, trending music) to share the senior's wisdom with a younger audience.


## 🛠️ Technical Specs for the Sandbox
- **Frontend:** React/Next.js with Tailwind CSS (configured for `text-lg` base).
- **Accessibility:** WCAG AAA Compliant (High contrast, screen reader ready).
- **Voice API:** ElevenLabs (for high-quality, emotive AI interviewing in multiple languages).
Use code with caution.
