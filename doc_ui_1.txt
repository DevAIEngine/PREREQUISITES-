The "Cinematic Legacy" Engine
1. The "National Geographic" Meets "Apple" Aesthetic
Visual Vibe: "Golden Hour" Cinematic. Instead of just cold "Cyberpunk Black," use deep, warm charcoal (#1a1a1a) mixed with rich gold (#FFD700) and deep teal accents. This feels premium, historical, and trustworthy (like a high-end museum exhibit).
Typography: Switch to a Geometric Sans-Serif (like Proxima Nova or Montserrat) which is trendy for youth but highly legible for seniors.
Rule: No font smaller than 18px anywhere. Headers should be massive (48px+) and act as art.
Imagery: Full-bleed, high-resolution backgrounds. The UI sits on top of the images like a "frosted glass" lens, ensuring the text is always readable against complex video backgrounds.
2. The "Multilingual" Core (US Top 5 Focus)
Dynamic Language Switching: The interface must instantly toggle between English, Spanish, Chinese (Simplified/Traditional), Tagalog, Vietnamese, and Arabic.
Audio-First Navigation: When a user switches to "Español," the entire AI voice interface switches to a native Spanish accent (e.g., a warm, friendly "abuela" tone or a respectful narrator tone).
Iconography: Use universally recognized symbols (a literal "Telephone" handset, a "Movie Camera") rather than abstract tech icons.
3. The "Voice-to-Docu" Engine (The Killer Feature)
The "Interview" Interface:
Instead of a "Form," this screen looks like an incoming video call.
Visual: A large, friendly AI Avatar (or a blank "Director's Chair" icon) that asks: "Tell me about your first car."
Action: A massive, pulsing "Answer Call" button (Green) and "End Session" (Red).
Youth Mode (The "Producer" View): If a younger user logs in, they see the same content but in a "Director's Cut" timeline where they can edit their grandparent's footage, add music, or share to TikTok/Instagram.
